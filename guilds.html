Mothers Union

The organization was founded by Mary Sumner in 1876 in the Church of England. She recalled her difficulties in child-care and training coupled with house chores and work place, and realized that mothers have enormous responsibilities. She was inspired to bring mothers of all social-classes together to provide support and training in motherhood so that they would be equipped to face the responsibilities in their marriage and family life. She taught young wives how to make good Christian home and bring up their children in the fear of God.

Fathers Union

Anglican Father’s Union (AFU) is a guild which was formed in 2007. Membership is growing slowly and there is need to encourage more fathers in all ecclesiastical divisions to join the guild.

Youth

The Anglican Youth Guild is a youth ministry in the Anglican Church. Membership is open to Anglican youth who are practicing Christians between the ages of 16 and 45. Youth Ministry is a group of vibrant youths who are determined to contribute a positive aura to the Church.

St. Veronica

St Veronica is passionate about grooming young women into becoming women of virtue either single or married. Women who uphold biblical principles in all their dealings. Helping them into becoming Christ like by word and deed

St. Agnes

Within the Youth Association, St Agnes Guild is a group of girls under the mentorship of the Mothers Union. Its objectives are to bring up these girls in a proper Christian way, where they are taught how to look after themselves as well as how to turn away from bad things.

St. Joseph

Within the Youth Association, arises St Joseph guild, which is there To serve God and Church by following Jesus and emulating the character of Saint Joseph, a carpenter by trade, husband of Mary and the earthly father of our Lord Jesus Christ.

Daughters of the King

The Order of the Daughters of the King is an order for women in the Anglican churches. Our Anglican tradition includes Episcopal oversight, recognizes that Holy Scripture contains all things necessary for salvation, acknowledges the gift of the Holy Spirit in Baptism, the real presence of our Lord in the Holy Eucharist, and worship according to The Book of Common Prayer. The Order’s purpose is to bring others into a living, loving relationship with Jesus our Lord and Savior and to help strengthen the spiritual life of our parishes and missions
